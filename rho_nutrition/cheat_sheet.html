The Individual Numbers — explained simply
Postscript SMS Activation GEORGE CONFIRMED
$900K–$1.8M/yr
1
George's store does roughly $14–16M/month in DTC sales on Shopify (80% of total revenue). Some people add products to their cart but never finish checking out. That's called cart abandonment — it happens about 70% of the time online.
2
If we send those people a text message ("Hey, you left something behind"), a small percentage come back and buy. The industry average for SMS cart recovery is 5–10%. We used 5% (the lowest, most conservative number).
3
We also add a text at the exact moment someone's subscription is about to renew — a "save" message. And a win-back text when someone cancels. All of this combined:
Roughly $75K–$150K in recovered revenue per month = $900K–$1.8M/year
If he asks: "how confident are you?" Very confident on the low end. This is the most certain number in the deck. The platform is already paid for. Every dollar from it is pure recovery. Even if we're off by 50%, it's still $450K+ in free money from a tool he's already paying for.
Pre-Renewal Klaviyo Flow Rebuild GEORGE CONFIRMED
$200K–$400K/yr
1
"I didn't get my renewal email" is the #2 most common CS ticket. George confirmed this. We estimate about 15% of all tickets are about this one issue.

If he asks: "where did 5,000 tickets/week come from?" George confirmed his CS team of 18 nearshore agents handles a very high volume. At a normal handle time of 5–7 minutes per ticket, 18 agents working full-time process roughly 4,000–7,000 tickets/week. We used 5,000 as our conservative estimate.

The Trustpilot score (1.7 stars) appears in Google search results when someone Googles "Rho Nutrition." Some people who would have bought see that score and leave. This is called conversion rate impact.
2
Rho's DTC store does about $14M/month on Shopify. If fixing the review score brings back even 0.1% more buyers — that's 1 out of every 1,000 website visitors who were on the fence:

Win-Back Sequence for Churned Subscribers INDUSTRY BENCHMARK
$620K–$1.2M/yr
1
About 33% of subscribers leave every month. That's standard for DTC supplement brands — George did not push back on this.
65,000 subscribers × 33% = ~21,500 people leave every month
2
Right now, when someone cancels, nothing happens. No email. No text. No offer. We send them a 3-message sequence — immediately when they cancel, 7 days later, and 30 days later with a small incentive (like a discount on their next order).
3
Industry win-back rates for e-commerce are 2–5%. We used 3% (middle of the range):
21,500 × 3% = 645 people come back per month
645 × $120 × 12 months = $928,800/year ≈ $930K
The range ($620K–$1.2M) uses 2% on the low end and 4% on the high end.
If he asks: "how do you know 3% is realistic?" That's the industry standard for subscription win-backs when you offer an incentive. Some brands get 5–8%. We used 3% to stay safe. The cost of sending those texts/emails is nearly zero.
