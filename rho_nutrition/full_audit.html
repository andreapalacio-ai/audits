Executive Summary

The product works. The operations don't.

Rho Nutrition has built a nine-figure DTC brand in under four years. The growth engine — paid media, product quality, and subscription conversion — is clearly working. What hasn't kept pace is everything that happens after the purchase.

At 65,000 active subscribers renewing every 28 days, the retention layer is the single highest-leverage area in the business. George has already identified this: his stated north star is moving average LTV from ~3 months to 4–5 months — the "escape velocity" threshold where subscribers have purchased enough products to stay indefinitely.

Every month that passes without fixing the retention and CS infrastructure is a month where roughly 21,500 subscribers churn — the majority of them silently, without a single automated attempt to save them or bring them back.

The opportunity is not to rebuild the business. It's to build the automation layer on top of what's already working — and capture the revenue that's currently falling through the gaps.

Deep Dive: Q1 Priority Opportunities

1. Postscript SMS — Activate What's Already Paid For
Cart recovery · Subscription churn-moment · Win-back triggers
Q1 — Week 1

George confirmed Postscript is under active contract and generating zero revenue. This is not a capability gap — it's an activation gap. The platform is already integrated, the contract is signed. The sequences just haven't been built.

What we build: A 3-touch abandoned cart sequence (1hr, 24hr, 72hr). A pre-churn SMS at the 28-day renewal moment ("Your order is about to renew — manage here"). A win-back trigger at day 0 of churn with a pull-forward offer. All connected to Retextion subscription data.

Why it's conservative: Cart recovery SMS industry benchmarks range from 5–15% recovery. At Rho's cart volume, even 5% recovery is significant. We've modeled at the low end.

2. Pre-Renewal Klaviyo Flow Rebuild
Eliminate #2 CS ticket driver · Reduce chargebacks · FTC compliance
Q1 — Week 1

"I didn't receive the renewal email" is the second-highest volume ticket category. Subscribers are being charged without warning, generating CS volume, chargebacks, and frustration — all of which feed the Trustpilot score problem.

What we build: A Klaviyo pre-renewal sequence triggered by Retextion subscription data: 7 days before charge (announcement + manage link), 3 days before (reminder with product image), 24 hours before (final notice). Each email includes a one-click manage/pause link. Deliverability-optimized with proper sender warming.

Compliance note: The FTC's 2023 updated negative option rules specifically require pre-renewal notification for subscription products. This isn't just good UX — it's regulatory protection.

3. Trustpilot Review Repair Flow
1.7 → 3.5+ in 60–90 days · Conversion rate recovery
Q1 — Week 2

The 1.7/5 Trustpilot score is visible in Google search results for every prospective customer searching "Rho Nutrition." This is a conversion problem — not primarily a reputation problem. It exists because the only customers who know Trustpilot exists are the ones who sought it out to complain. Happy customers don't go looking for review platforms.

What we build: A post-delivery Klaviyo fork triggered 7 days after confirmed delivery. This ensures the review distribution reflects the actual customer experience rather than a selection bias of frustrated customers.

Target: 3.5+ score is achievable within 60–90 days at current subscriber volume. Each point of Trustpilot score improvement has a measurable impact on Google SERP appearance and DTC conversion rate.

5. Post-Purchase LTV Extension Sequence
The single highest-leverage lever in the business
Q2

George's stated north star is extending average LTV from ~3 months to 4–5 months. There is currently no systematic mechanism to make this happen. Subscribers either renew out of habit or churn — there's no structured education, no deliberate cross-sell, and no data-driven personalization in the post-purchase journey.

What we build: A structured post-purchase Klaviyo sequence: Day 7 (product education, how to get results), Day 14 (social proof from customers like them, introduce second product category), Day 21 (cross-sell trigger pointing toward escape velocity products). Personalized by health goal data from the questionnaire (Opportunity 9). Connected to purchase history to avoid recommending products already subscribed.

10. 24/7 AI Phone Agent
George said "I will consider phone" — this is the opening
Q2

George explicitly opened the door on phone support in the discovery call: "I will consider phone." A 24/7 AI phone agent handles the top 3 ticket categories autonomously — order lookup, subscription cancellation save attempts, and tracking inquiries — without adding headcount.

What we build: An AI voice agent (Bland AI, Vapi, or Synthflow) connected to Shopify order data and Retextion subscription API. Handles inbound calls: "What is your order number?" → pulls data → answers tracking, status, renewal questions in real time. Why this is the right time: After Q1 systems are live, the top ticket categories will be largely deflected digitally. Phone volume is the residual — and the highest-value interactions. This is where a save attempt on a phone call can recover a $120/month subscriber.

9. Customer Health Goal Questionnaire
Personalization engine for all downstream Klaviyo flows
Q2

Rho Nutrition sells science-backed nutrition products. The customers who stay longest are the ones who see results. The customers who churn are the ones who don't know how to use the product correctly or don't understand which products match their health goals. A customer goal questionnaire at onboarding captures this intent and routes it into every subsequent communication.

What we build: A Typeform questionnaire triggered post-purchase (embedded in welcome email + SMS). Captures: primary health goal, experience level, products already using. Webhook pushes responses to Shopify customer notes and Klaviyo profile properties. Every downstream flow (education, cross-sell, win-back) then personalizes based on these tags. The customer who wants "performance" gets a different Day 14 email than the customer who wants "recovery."
